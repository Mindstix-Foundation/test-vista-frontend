Section D: Case Study-based Question (5 marks)
Read the following case study and answer the questions below:

In a school science experiment, a group of students was asked to investigate the effect of light intensity on the rate of photosynthesis in plants. They used a plant in a beaker of water, with a lamp placed at different distances from the plant. The students observed the number of oxygen bubbles released as a measure of the photosynthesis rate.

Questions: [5 Marks]
1. What is the independent variable in this experiment?
2. What would be the expected result if the light intensity is increased?
3. Why is oxygen production used as an indicator of photosynthesis?
4. What are the factors affecting the rate of photosynthesis?
5. How would the experiment change if the temperature was increased?
